# Code of Conduct

TinyPyk is a learning project for children, families, educators, and
contributors. The community should be warm, respectful, and safe.

## Expected Behavior

- Be kind and patient.
- Assume good intent, but correct problems clearly.
- Use inclusive, age-appropriate language.
- Keep discussions focused on learning, safety, accessibility, and code.
- Respect maintainers' decisions about child safety and scope.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, bullying, threats, or discriminatory language.
- Sexual, violent, hateful, or frightening content.
- Asking children for private information.
- Encouraging unsupervised child-to-child contact.
- Publishing secrets, private data, or exploit details.
- Pushing AI or voice behavior that is not suitable for young learners.

## Child-Safety Standard

TinyPyk contributors should treat child safety as more important than feature
speed. If a feature could expose a child to unsafe content, private contact,
tracking, or pressure to share personal information, it needs redesign before
it belongs in TinyPyk.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, close, or remove issues, pull requests, comments, or
contributions that conflict with this code of conduct. Serious or repeated
problems may lead to blocking from the project.
